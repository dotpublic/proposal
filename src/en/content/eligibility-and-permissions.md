# Eligibility and permissions: {.app-content-centered}
## Framework for services on .PUBLIC {.app-content-centered}

This DRAFT framework will be designed to enable .PUBLIC to function not just as a permissioned space, but as a civic contract.

### 1. Purpose and mission:
Why is this institution permitted to operate under .PUBLIC?

**Requirements:**
Organisations must demonstrate clear, verifiable institutional ownership and mission.
- Clear declaration of institutional ownership and legal identity
- Transparent organisational structure and funding sources
- Named editorial leads or accountable officers
- Permanent public record of accreditation and changes in status

**Oversight functions:**
- Regular reviews of organisational status and compliance
- Public reporting on accreditation and oversight activities
- Verification audits at regular intervals
- Public-facing directory of .PUBLIC publishers
- Notification and appeals process for third-party challenges to provenance

**Disqualification trigger:**
- Failure to maintain a true and permanent public record of ownership, accreditation, unclear institutional authorship.

### 2. Accessibility and inclusion:
Who can access this resource, and under what terms?

**Requirements:**
Organisations must ensure universal access to core services and information.
- Universal digital accessibility in compliance with [WCAG] standards
- Multilingual provision where appropriate to jurisdiction or remit
- Human-readable summaries of legal and data terms
- No registration walls for essential information
- Pro-active design for access by vulnerable or low-connectivity groups (low-bandwidth environments and/or assistive technologies)

### Oversight functions:

- Spot-checks for compliance with accessibility standards
- Annual access audits by third-party assessors
- Inclusion scorecards or public feedback channels

**Disqualification trigger:**
- Failure to provide multilingual and accessible information, use of registration walls for essential services

### 3. Provenance and attribution: What is this source, and how do we know?
**Requirements:**
Organisations must be publicly accountable for the purpose, intent, provenance, accuracy, and transparency of what they share.
- Stated organisational mission or objective, whether commercial, non-profit, civic, or ideological (to Inform, Educate or Entertain)
- Authorship and publication metadata embedded in each resource
- Formal agreement to uphold .PUBLIC obligations when publishing to the domain
- Evident willingness to account for the sources, reasoning, and accuracy of published claims
- No capitalisation of .PUBLIC status, nor any misleading implication of endorsement by the .PUBLIC network

### Oversight functions:
- Published audits of adherence to .PUBLIC publishing standards
- Independent review of applications based on transparency, not ideology or intent
- Public correction and dispute resolution mechanisms for demonstrable falsehoods, omissions, or misleading interpretation
- Revocation protocols for institutions persistently unwilling to meet minimum accountability thresholds

### Disqualification trigger:
- Refusal to formally uphold .PUBLIC obligations when publishing, demonstrable unwillingness to correct misinformation once publicised, absence of embedded publication metadata.

### 4. Editorial integrity: Can this information be relied on, and is it communicated responsibly?

**Requirements:**
Organisations must demonstrate ethical communication and editorial standards.
- Clear editorial policies published and adhered to
- Separation of commentary and fact
- Transparent correction processes
- No automated amplification systems driven by engagement metrics
- Ethical moderation practices where user interaction is allowed

**Oversight functions:**
- Community flagging and dispute resolution mechanisms
- Monitoring of correction response rates
- Independent editorial review board for high-risk domains

**Disqualification trigger:**
- Failure to distinguish fact from commentary, absence of clear correction processes, unresolved ethical concerns around external influence.

### 5. Data handling and privacy: How is data collected, stored, and used?
**Requirements:**
Organisations must operate under the highest standard of data protection.
- GDPR or equivalent compliance regardless of jurisdiction
- Plain-language privacy policies
- No tracking or fingerprinting for behavioural exploitation
- Secure authentication mechanisms if PII are used
- Strict rules for secure data retention and sharing

**Oversight functions:**
- Data practice audits by trusted third-party organisations
- Zero-tolerance enforcement
- Annual data impact assessments published in open format

**Disqualification trigger:**
- Use of behavioural tracking technologies, insufficient disclosure of data practices, lack of accessible privacy documentation.

### 6. Operational transparency and legal compliance: 
Is this institution open about how it works and legally sound?

**Requirements:**
Organisations must be open and accountable in their operations.
- Open publication of key institutional policies (editorial, hiring, procurement)
- Public annual report on .PUBLIC obligations
- Compliance with legislation, but in ways that do not breach .PUBLIC principles
- Willingness to submit to arbitration in cases of legal or civic conflict

**Oversight functions:**
- Publicly accessible repository of public accountability documents
- Mechanism for whistle-blowers or external alerts
- Red-flag list for at-risk institutions under review

**Disqualification trigger:**
- Breach of .PUBLIC transparency obligations, failure to disclose key institutional policies, unwillingness to engage with third-party accountability mechanisms.

### 7. Contribution to the Dot-Public value network: Does the institution enhance the collective health of the .PUBLIC domain?
**Requirements:**
- Organisations are encouraged to contribute to the collective health of the civic internet.
- Use of persistent identifiers and FAIR data principles
- Commitment to publishing under open civic-Ai-compliant licenses
- Participation in peer review, standard setting, or cross-institutional projects
- Shared stewardship of common services like archives, directories, or metadata schema

**Oversight functions:**

- Contribution to indexing (non-punitive but incentivised)
- Federation-level metrics on public value generated
- Tiered participation models for core vs. peripheral actors

**Disqualification trigger:**
- Neglect of FAIR data standards, absence of open licensing.

The oversight bodies e.g., the 'council of standards and ethics, would be responsible for future-facing, adapting, and applying these rules to ensure relevance, consistency and fairness.
